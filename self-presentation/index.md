The first work I'll show you was created for a typography class. The assignment was to design a propaganda poster with complete creative freedom, but I chose to avoid direct political messaging and instead focused on health, creating a fictional campaign about junk food and its impact on the body.

My goal was rather than realism, create design that has a strong visual impact to communicate its message.

![Second project - Frankfurt book festival visual identity](img/frankfurt.png)

The second project was created during graphic design studio classes and focused on a more complex task, and that is designing a visual identity for the Frankfurt Book Fair, with the theme Czechia ~ Land on the Coast. 

As we all (hopefully) know the Czech Republic has no coastline, so I approached the topic conceptually rather than literally. I worked with paper as a symbol of the book and watercolor as a metaphor for water and coast, allowing color to flow naturally across the surface.

The result is a colorful visual system built around three main festival colors, combined with modern typography and a clear layout structure.
